Project 1: Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii Photo Collection

Overview

The task of this project is to automatically align and composite these separate channel images to produce a single RGB color image.

Results

Below are the successfully aligned and colorized images from the Prokudin-Gorskii collection. Each image shows the result of aligning the red and green channels to the blue channel as reference.

My Choice Pictures

These are additional images I selected from the Prokudin-Gorskii collection to test the alignment algorithm. They present different challenges including varying textures, architectural details, and natural scenes.

Challenges & Solutions

The most challenging aspect was aligning the Emir photograph, where the different color channels had significantly different brightness values for the clothing. I first tried the zero mean approach, and then used edge detection preprocessing to solve this issue. Adjusting the window size of the pyramid also helped to make more precise alignment.

The pyramid approach I developed was also essential for processing high-resolution .tif files efficiently, reducing computation time from several minutes to under 30 seconds (on MacBook Pro) per image while maintaining alignment accuracy.
